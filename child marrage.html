What is child marriage

  • Anurag Kumar
  • Jan 1, 2023
  • 12 Comments

Child marriage is a marriage in which one or both parties are children, usually under the age of 18. It is a violation of human rights and a form of child abuse, as it deprives children of their childhood, education, and opportunities for personal growth and development. Child marriage can have devastating consequences for the physical, emotional, and mental health of children, as well as their overall well-being. It is a global issue that affects millions of girls and boys every year, particularly in developing countries where poverty, cultural practices, and gender inequality are prevalent.

How to controll child marriage ?

  • Anurag Kumar
  • Jan 1, 2023
  • 12 Comments

Controlling child marriage requires a comprehensive and multi-faceted approach that involves government, civil society organizations, religious leaders, communities, and families. Here are some strategies that can be effective in preventing and reducing child marriage: Education: Education is one of the most effective ways to prevent child marriage. Providing access to quality education for girls and boys can help them develop the skills and knowledge they need to make informed decisions about their lives and future. Enforce laws: Governments should enforce laws that prohibit child marriage and ensure that perpetrators are held accountable. This includes establishing age limits for marriage, increasing penalties for violators, and providing support services for victims. Empower girls: Empowering girls through education, life skills training, and economic opportunities can help them resist the pressures to marry early and pursue their aspirations. Awareness-raising: Raising awareness about the harms of child marriage through public campaigns, community outreach, and engagement with religious and traditional leaders can help change attitudes and social norms. Support families: Providing economic and social support to families can reduce the economic incentives for child marriage and help parents prioritize their children's education and well-being. Strengthen child protection systems: Strengthening child protection systems, including child welfare services and legal aid, can help prevent and respond to cases of child marriage. Collaboration: Collaboration among stakeholders, including governments, civil society organizations, and international agencies, can help coordinate efforts to prevent and respond to child marriage.

What are the effects of child marriage?.

  • Anurag Kumar
  • Jan 1, 2023
  • 12 Comments

Child marriage can have serious and long-lasting effects on the physical, emotional, and psychological well-being of children. Here are some of the effects of child marriage: Health risks: Child brides are at increased risk of health complications related to early pregnancy and childbirth, including obstructed labor, maternal mortality, and fistula. Education: Child marriage often forces children, especially girls, to drop out of school, which can limit their opportunities for personal growth and economic empowerment. Economic disadvantage: Child brides are often economically disadvantaged, which can lead to a cycle of poverty and dependence on their spouses. Violence and abuse: Child brides are more likely to experience domestic violence and sexual abuse by their spouses, who may be much older and have more power in the relationship. Mental health: Child brides often experience depression, anxiety, and other mental health problems due to the stress and trauma of being forced into marriage and the challenges they face in their new roles as wives and mothers. Social isolation: Child brides may be socially isolated from their peers and communities, which can lead to feelings of loneliness and despair. Limited life choices: Child marriage limits children's life choices and opportunities for personal and professional development, and perpetuates gender inequality and discrimination. Overall, child marriage is a violation of children's rights and a form of child abuse that can have serious and lasting consequences for their well-being and future prospects.

How is Save the Children is a world leader in ending child marriage?

  • Anurag Kumar
  • Jan 1, 2023
  • 12 Comments

Save the Children is a global leader in the fight to end child marriage. The organization works in over 100 countries around the world to prevent child marriage, support girls who are at risk, and advocate for policy change. Here are some of the ways Save the Children is working to end child marriage: Prevention: Save the Children works with communities, families, and girls themselves to prevent child marriage by raising awareness about the harms of the practice, promoting girls' education and empowerment, and providing economic opportunities for families. Protection: Save the Children works to protect girls who are at risk of child marriage by providing counseling and support services, as well as legal assistance to prevent early marriages and protect the rights of girls. Advocacy: Save the Children advocates for policy change at the national and international levels to end child marriage, including pushing for stronger laws and policies that protect girls' rights, and engaging with religious and traditional leaders to change attitudes and social norms. Research and Data: Save the Children conducts research and collects data on child marriage to better understand the problem and develop effective solutions. Collaboration: Save the Children collaborates with other organizations, governments, and stakeholders to end child marriage, including working with partners to develop and implement programs and initiatives aimed at ending the practice. Overall, Save the Children is a world leader in ending child marriage, and has made significant progress in preventing and responding to child marriage around the world.
